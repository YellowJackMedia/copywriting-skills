# Scarcity Stacking

Layer multiple types of scarcity to create compounding urgency. One type of scarcity is weak. Three types together are irresistible.

## Why It Works

A single scarcity trigger (e.g., "limited time") can be rationalized away. But when you stack multiple independent limitations, each one reinforces the others. The reader's brain shifts from "should I?" to "can I?" — and "can I?" is a much harder question to answer with "no."

## The Five Types of Scarcity

| Type | Mechanism | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Time** | Deadline or countdown | "Offer closes Friday at midnight" |
| **Quantity** | Limited supply | "Only 12 spots remaining" |
| **Access** | Exclusive channel | "Only available through this email" |
| **Social proof** | Others are acting | "340 people signed up in the first 4 hours" |
| **Irreversibility** | Can't come back | "Once removed, this pricing is permanent" |

## The Framework

1. **Pick 2-3 types** that fit your offer (don't use all 5 — it feels fake)
2. **Lead with the strongest** (usually time or quantity)
3. **Layer the second as confirmation** ("And even if you had time, there are only...")
4. **Layer the third as the closer** ("And this won't be available through any other channel")

## Template

```
[Primary scarcity: TIME]
"When this timer hits zero / this offer closes on [date], it's gone."

[Secondary scarcity: QUANTITY]
"And even if you're reading this before the deadline, there are only [X] spots / units / licenses."

[Tertiary scarcity: ACCESS or IRREVERSIBILITY]
"This pricing / access / bonus is ONLY available [channel]. After this, it's [full price / invite-only / gone forever]."
```

## Examples

**Course launch:**
"Registration closes Sunday at midnight — no extensions. We're capping this cohort at 40 students because our instructors can't give proper feedback beyond that. And this early-bird pricing ($297 instead of $497) is only in this email. Monday morning, it goes back to full price for everyone."

**Service offer:**
"I can only take on 3 new clients this quarter — my team is at capacity. If you book before the 15th, I'll include the audit at no charge (normally $1,500). After the 15th, it's full price and you're on the waitlist."

**Event:**
"Early bird ends Friday. We've already sold 85 of 120 seats. And the VIP dinner with the speakers? That's capped at 20 — and 14 spots are already taken."

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Fake scarcity kills trust.** If you say "only 10 spots" and then offer 50 more, you're done.
- ❌ **Don't stack all 5 types** — 2-3 is the sweet spot. More than that feels manipulative.
- ❌ **Don't make scarcity your only selling point** — the offer has to be genuinely valuable first
- ❌ **Never extend deadlines without consequence** — if you extend, explain why and make the extension worse

## Combine With

- **Binary Identity** — "Type A grabs the last spot. Type B checks back Monday and it's gone."
- **Exclusivity** — "This is only for members. Non-members can't access it at any price."
- **Future Pacing** — Show what the people who grabbed the last spots are doing in 6 months
