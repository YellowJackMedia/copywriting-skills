# Future Pacing

Paint a vivid, specific picture of what the reader's life looks like after they take action. Make them feel the result before they buy.

## Why It Works

People don't buy products — they buy the version of themselves that exists after using the product. Future pacing activates the same neural pathways as actually experiencing the outcome. The more sensory and specific the description, the more real it feels.

## The Framework

1. **Start with "Imagine..." or "Picture this..."** — invites the reader into the scene
2. **Set a specific time** — "It's Tuesday morning, three months from now"
3. **Describe sensory details** — what they see, hear, feel
4. **Show the contrast with their current state** — "Instead of [current pain], you're [future gain]"
5. **Make it feel inevitable** — not "you could" but "you will"

## Template

```
Picture this: It's [specific time], [time period] from now.

You're [specific scene]. [Sensory detail]. [Emotional detail].

[Specific result they're experiencing]. [How others react].

[Contrast with current state: "Three months ago, you were..."]

The only thing standing between you and this reality is [action].
```

## Examples

**Service pitch (welding/facilities):**
"Picture this: It's Monday morning, six weeks from now. You walk into the shop and every machine is running. No red tags. No emergency calls. Your foreman hands you the weekly report — zero downtime, all projects on schedule. You grab your coffee and actually get to work on growing the business instead of putting out fires.

Right now, you're probably dealing with at least one piece of equipment that's been 'almost broken' for weeks. That's the one that's going to go down during your biggest job."

**Product launch:**
"Imagine opening your laptop on a Tuesday. Your content calendar for the next 30 days is fully planned. The first three pieces are already drafted. Your analytics dashboard shows organic traffic up 40% from last month. And you spent less than two hours on all of it.

That's not a fantasy. That's what happens when you stop writing content and start building a content system."

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Don't be vague ("you'll be successful") — be specific ("you'll close 3 more deals this month")
- ❌ Don't future pace without grounding it in current reality first
- ❌ Don't promise things your product can't deliver
- ❌ Don't overdo it — one future pace per piece is enough

## Combine With

- **Parallel Narrative** — Future pace Character A, present-pace Character B
- **Binary Identity** — "Type A is already living this. Type B never will."
- **Scarcity** — "This future is available to the next 10 people who..."
