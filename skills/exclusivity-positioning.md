# Exclusivity Positioning

Make the reader feel selected, not sold to. The best customers don't chase deals — they're invited.

## Why It Works

When something is available to everyone, it feels cheap. When it's only available to a select few, it feels valuable — even if the product is identical. Exclusivity activates status-seeking instincts. People don't want what everyone has. They want what only a few have.

## The Framework

1. **Define the in-group** — who gets access (use specific, aspirational criteria)
2. **Quantify the exclusion** — "Only 2%" or "Only 100 spots" — make the barrier feel real
3. **Describe what the in-group gets** that others don't (access, community, results)
4. **Make the reader prove they qualify** — application, invitation, or criteria check
5. **Never pitch to the excluded** — make them want in, then let them qualify themselves

## Template

```
This isn't for everyone.

[Specific criteria for who it IS for].
[Specific criteria for who it ISN'T for].

[What the in-group experiences].
[What the excluded miss out on].

[How to join — make it feel like earning a spot, not buying a product].
```

## Examples

**Premium service:**
"We don't take every client who calls. Our minimum engagement is $5,000/month because we only work with businesses doing $1M+ in revenue who are serious about scaling. If you're still figuring out your product-market fit, we're not the right fit either. But if you're ready to build a marketing engine that runs without you — let's talk."

**Membership/community:**
"The Skunkworks isn't advertised. You find it through a member, or you don't find it at all. We have 200 members across 14 countries. Average net worth is $2.3M. The waiting list is 6 months. Current acceptance rate: 8%."

**Content/free resource:**
"I only share my Google Ads scripts with people who've read the full article and implemented at least one thing manually first. The scripts are powerful, but they're useless if you don't understand what they're automating. If that's you, the link is at the bottom."

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Don't fake exclusivity** — if everyone can click "Buy Now," it's not exclusive
- ❌ **Don't make the criteria too narrow** — you still need customers
- ❌ **Don't be condescending** — "This isn't for losers" isn't exclusivity, it's arrogance
- ❌ **Don't use exclusivity for commodity products** — it only works when there's genuine differentiation

## Combine With

- **Binary Identity** — "Type A qualifies. Type B doesn't. Yet."
- **Scarcity** — "We accept 10 new members per quarter. This quarter, 6 spots are already taken."
- **Future Pacing** — "Imagine being in a room with 199 other operators who've all built $1M+ businesses..."
- **Parallel Narrative** — The person who got in vs. the person on the waiting list
